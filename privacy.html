1. Overview

Credit Card Stacker is an educational credit card planning app designed to help users understand credit utilization, statement dates, payment reminders, card categories, and general credit card habits. This Privacy Policy explains how we handle information when you use the app.

In the current MVP/demo version, the app stores most information locally in your browser using localStorage. That means the information is saved on your device/browser unless you clear it, export it, or delete it through the app settings.

Credit Card Stacker is not a bank, lender, credit bureau, credit repair company, or financial advisor. The app does not guarantee approval, credit score changes, or better credit terms.

2. Data We Collect

Depending on which features you use, the app may collect or store the following types of information:

Data Type Examples Purpose
Account information Name, email address, demo sign-in status To personalize the app and connect your profile to saved settings.
Credit profile inputs Estimated score range, income range, recent inquiry range, main credit goal To provide educational guidance and card category suggestions.
Card tracking information Card name, balance, credit limit, statement date, payment due date To calculate utilization, reminders, payoff targets, and reports.
Learning activity Lesson progress, quiz answers, checklist progress To show progress and help users learn credit basics.
Premium/demo billing status Demo premium active status, selected plan type To control feature access in demo mode and prepare for real billing later.
Privacy and reminder settings Reminder preferences, personalization choices, disclosure settings To respect user preferences and customize the app experience.

Information we do not collect in the MVP

  • We do not collect Social Security numbers.
  • We do not pull full credit reports.
  • We do not perform hard credit checks.
  • We do not connect to bank accounts in the current demo version.
  • We do not store real payment card numbers in this HTML app.

3. How We Use Data

We use information to provide and improve app features, including:

  • Calculating credit utilization based on user-entered balances and limits.
  • Creating educational payoff targets and reminder timelines.
  • Showing beginner-friendly card categories based on user-selected profile ranges.
  • Saving app preferences such as reminders, privacy settings, and premium demo status.
  • Generating reports that users can copy, print, or download.
  • Helping users understand credit concepts through lessons, quizzes, and checklists.

We do not use your information to make lending decisions. Any card category or educational suggestion is not an approval decision and should not be treated as financial advice.

4. Sharing and Third Parties

In the current front-end demo version, data is stored locally in your browser and is not sent to a server by this page. If the app later connects to Supabase, Stripe, credit bureau APIs, analytics tools, or affiliate card networks, this policy must be updated to clearly explain those providers.

Possible future providers

  • Supabase: for authentication, user profiles, and saved app data.
  • Stripe: for subscription checkout and billing portal access.
  • Credit data provider: only if real credit score access is added later with user consent.
  • Affiliate networks: only if real credit card offers are added later.
  • Analytics provider: only if enabled and disclosed in the app.
If affiliate links are added, the app should clearly disclose that compensation may be earned and that approval is not guaranteed.

5. Storage and Security

In the demo version, information is stored in browser localStorage. This is useful for testing but is not the same as a secure production database. Anyone with access to the same browser profile may be able to view or clear local data.

For a production version, user account data should be protected with secure authentication, database row-level security, encrypted transport, and server-side controls. Sensitive API keys must never be placed inside public HTML or JavaScript files.

  • Do not store Stripe secret keys in front-end files.
  • Do not store Supabase service-role keys in browser code.
  • Do not store Social Security numbers in the MVP.
  • Use server-side functions for billing, webhooks, and sensitive API calls.

6. Your Choices and Controls

Users should have clear control over their information. The app includes settings for:

  • Changing profile inputs and estimated score ranges.
  • Turning reminders on or off.
  • Controlling educational personalization.
  • Exporting saved app data.
  • Deleting saved card data.
  • Deleting all local app data from the browser.

Credit Card Stacker is not designed for children under 13. Users should not submit personal information if they are under 13. If this app later becomes available in app stores, add age screening and comply with all child privacy requirements that apply to your audience and location.
