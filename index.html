What it does

A credit card strategy app, not another boring score dashboard.

The goal is to help users understand what action to take next: pay down, wait, apply, or avoid applying too soon.

💳

Card stacking plan

Users enter their score range, income range, current cards, balances, and limits. The app creates a simple path for what type of card to consider next.

📉

Utilization calculator

Shows current utilization and how much to pay to reach common targets like under 30%, under 10%, or close to 1%.

⏰

Statement reminders

Reminds users to pay before the statement closing date, which is often more useful than only tracking the due date.

🧠

Beginner explanations

Explains credit terms in plain English: utilization, hard inquiries, secured cards, annual fees, APR, and payment history.

🛡️

No hard pull MVP

The first version uses manual score ranges instead of pulling private credit reports. This keeps the app easier and safer to launch.

⚖️

Clear disclaimers

The app does not guarantee approval or score increases. It gives educational guidance and keeps recommendations transparent.

FAQ

Questions users will ask.

Does this app pull my real credit score?

Not in the starter version. Users manually enter an estimated score range. A real credit-score connection can be added later through a credit bureau or approved data provider.

Will this hurt my credit?

The basic app does not run a credit check. If real score access is added later, the app should clearly explain whether it is a soft inquiry and ask for user consent first.

Does the app guarantee approval?

No. Lenders make approval decisions. The app can show general card categories that may fit a user profile, but approval should never be promised.

Is this credit repair?

No. The app is designed as an educational credit card planning tool. It should not claim to remove negative items, dispute credit reports for users, or guarantee score changes.
